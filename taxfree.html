Tax Efficient Donations

Donating to charity is great for philanthropic reasons, but did you know it can also help lower your taxes? Each year the IRS allows you to donate a certain amount to charity to offset a portion of your taxable income.

Over 70.5 Years young?

The IRS has deemed 70.5 a very important year in your life. Once you reach this age, you are required to withdraw a certain amount from your Individual Retirement Account (IRA), known as your Required Minimum Distribtuion (RMD(). The amount varies depending on the balance of the account but is mandatory. Failure to withdraw your RMD can cost you. Uncle Sam will tax you at 50% of your RMD amount if it is not distributed.

For those of you who have properly saved for retirement, your RMD can seem excessive and unwanted. If that is you, donating a portion of your RMD can be an effective tool to lower your taxable income. With the current administration new tax policy, donating to charity from your IRA has become extremely popular for the tax benefits.
